Questions to Ask Potential Contractors During the Selection Process

When you're in the process of selecting a roofing contractor, there are a few key questions that you'll want to ask in order to ensure that you're choosing a reliable and trustworthy company. Here are a few of the most important questions to ask:

1. How long has the company been in business?

You'll want to make sure that you're working with a company that has plenty of experience and a good track record. A company that's been in business for many years is likely to be more reliable than a newer company.

2. Does the company have insurance?

It's important to make sure that your roofing contractor has insurance, as this will protect you in case of any accidents or damages during the project. Be sure to ask for proof of insurance before hiring a contractor.

3. Are the contractors licensed and certified?

In order to work on your roof, contractors must be properly licensed and certified. This ensures that they have the proper training and qualifications for the job. Ask to see proof of certification before hiring a contractor.

Check Reputation and Reviews of Companies Before Making a Selection

When considering hiring a roofing company, be sure to check their reputation and reviews. You can do this by searching online, asking for referrals from people you trust, or checking with the Better Business Bureau.

It’s important to read reviews from multiple sources to get a well-rounded idea of what others think of the company. If you only read positive reviews, you may not be getting the full story. Likewise, if you only read negative reviews, you may not be fair to the company.

When looking at reviews, pay attention to patterns. For example, if multiple people mention that the customer service was poor or that the job wasn’t done well, that’s something to take into consideration. On the other hand, if most of the reviews are positive, that’s a good sign.

Another thing to consider is how responsive the company is to complaints. If they seem to address complaints in a professional and timely manner, that’s a good sign. However, if they ignore complaints or try to brush them under the rug, that’s not a good sign.

Finally, when talking to people who have used the company before, ask about their overall experience. Were they happy with the work? Would they recommend the company to others? If most people say they were happy with the work and would recommend the company, that’s a good sign.
