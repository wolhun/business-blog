Why is Office 365 tenant to tenant migration required?

Microsoft Office 365 is a sophisticated cloud platform and one of the best email platforms available. The majority of businesses have been switching over to this platform for a long time. Let's take a look at the common reasons why tenant to tenant Office 365 migration is required before delving into the complexities of tenant to tenant migration.

  1. Mergers and acquisitions: Companies merge, acquire, or divest business units that were established independently. Old mailboxes must be deleted and replaced with new ones. This can sometimes result in company domain name changes, which is why there are an increasing number of Office 365 to Office 365 migration projects.

    For example, consider a company that uses Office 365 as its email platform. It now acquires another smaller company also using Office 365 as its email platform. The larger company that acquired the smaller, now wants to consolidate all the IT systems into a single domain. This necessitates the transfer of users, data, and domains into the new tenant.

  1. Data localization and regional compliances: Every organization has its own set of compliance regulations that must be followed. However, when an organization operates in multiple countries, the laws in another country may differ significantly from the country the business is operating from. This necessitates that the user's Office 365 services hosted on servers in the specific country be brought back to the home country where the business is located to comply with the local laws.

  1. For backing up Office 365 data in the cloud: Organizations that do not want to backup their data on-premises can use tenant-to-tenant migration to backup their email in the cloud service before storing it elsewhere. Assume a company has two Office 365 subscriptions. It can migrate data from one Office 365 account to another, and the migrated data serves as a cloud backup copy. A second basic Office 365 account license can serve as a better cloud backup than overpriced hard-disk storage.

Challenges involved in Office 365 tenant to tenant migration

Migration from Office 365 to Office 365 tenant appears to be more difficult than migration from on-premise to Office 365, because Microsoft allows a domain to exist in only one Office tenant at a time.

Assume you currently use domainX.com in Office 365 tenant X and want to migrate this domain, mailboxes, and users to Office 365 tenant Y. You must delete or rename user accounts, mailboxes, Office 365 Groups, and so on from domain X before adding it to tenant Y. You can remove domain X from tenant X after renaming the objects in domain X. This necessitates moving to a different domain like xxx.onmicrosoft.com where the Office 365 platform detects that domain X is not associated with any tenant. Only after that can you register it with tenant Y and create a mailbox on that domain.
