Researching and Interviewing Potential Property Lawyers

Anyone can call themselves a property lawyer, but not everyone has the experience and expertise to provide quality legal services. When you're selecting a property lawyer, it's important to do your research to find someone who is reputable and qualified to handle your case.

Here are some tips for researching and interviewing potential property lawyers:

Check qualifications: Make sure the lawyer you're considering is qualified to practice law in your state or territory. In Queensland, property lawyers must be admitted to practice law by the Supreme Court of Queensland.

Ask about experience: Find out how long the lawyer has been practising property law, and ask about their experience with cases like yours. For example, if you're buying a property, ask if they've handled any other purchases in the past.

Get referrals: Talk to friends, family, or other business professionals who have used a property lawyer in the past. They can give you first-hand insights into what it's like to work with that lawyer.

Check online reviews: Take some time to read online reviews of potential lawyers. Look for reviews from clients as well as other lawyers. Pay attention to both positive and negative reviews so you can get a well-rounded view of each lawyer's practice.

Interview several lawyers: Once you've narrowed down your list of potential lawyers, set up appointments for initial consultations. This will give you a chance to learn more about each lawyer's experience and approach to handling cases like yours.

Asking the Right Questions When Selecting A Property Lawyer

Choosing a property lawyer can be a tricky task, especially if you're not sure what to look for. However, by asking the right questions, you can ensure that you select a reputable and experienced lawyer who can help you with your specific needs. Here are four important questions to ask when choosing a property lawyer:

1. What experience do you have in handling property law matters?

You want to choose a property lawyer who has extensive experience in dealing with all aspects of property law. This includes issues such as commercial leasing, conveyancing, strata title disputes, and development applications. Ask about the lawyer's specific experience and whether they have handled any cases similar to yours.

2. Can you provide me with references from past clients?

A good property lawyer should be able to provide you with references from past clients. These references can give you an insight into the quality of the lawyer's work and how satisfied their clients have been with the outcome of their matter.

3. What are your fees for legal services?

It's important to know what fees the lawyer charges for their services before engaging them. Ask about their hourly rates and whether they offer fixed-fee options for certain services. Get an estimate of the total costs involved so that there are no surprises down the track.

4. Do you have insurance cover for professional indemnity and public liability?

All reputable lawyers should have insurance cover for professional indemnity and public liability.
