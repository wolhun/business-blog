Common Roofing Materials Used by Plymouth, MN Companies

By Nathaniel Carter · 31 August 2024 · 5 min read

Asphalt Shingles

Composition and Types

Asphalt shingles are one of the most popular roofing materials in Plymouth, MN. They consist of a fiberglass base that is coated with asphalt and granules. These shingles come in two primary types: 3-tab shingles and architectural shingles. Three-tab shingles are flat and have a simple look, making them a cost-effective choice. Architectural shingles, on the other hand, are thicker and offer a more dimensional appearance.

When choosing asphalt shingles, consider the local weather. In Plymouth, the winter can be harsh, so opting for a durable brand can make a difference. Midwest Construction and Remodeling often recommends certain types that withstand snow and ice better than others.

Benefits and Drawbacks

The key benefits of asphalt shingles include affordability, ease of installation, and variety of styles. They can fit nearly any home aesthetic and come in numerous colors. Asphalt shingles also have good fire resistance, which is crucial for safety.

However, they do have some drawbacks. They may not last as long as metal or tile roofs, usually needing replacement after 15 to 30 years. They can also be prone to damage from high winds and severe weather, which is something to consider if you're living in Plymouth.

Installation Process

Installing asphalt shingles requires careful preparation. First, the old roofing material must be removed, and the roof deck must be inspected. Then, a layer of underlayment is applied to prevent water leakage. Afterward, shingles are nailed down, starting from the bottom and working upwards, often in a staggered pattern.

Midwest Construction and Remodeling has skilled professionals who ensure an efficient installation process. This guarantees that homeowners receive a quality roof that will protect their home for many years.

Popular Brands in Plymouth, MN

In Plymouth, MN, several brands of asphalt shingles are popular. CertainTeed and Owens Corning are among the most preferred names for their durability and warranty options. Brands like GAF are also well-regarded for both their aesthetic options and performance in extreme weather conditions.

Because Midwest Construction and Remodeling works closely with these manufacturers, they can guide you on which brand fits your needs best, ensuring a roofing system that is both functional and visually pleasing.

Metal Roofing

Types of Metal Roofing (Steel, Aluminum, Copper)

Metal roofing is becoming increasingly popular in Plymouth, MN, for its durability and modern look. Three common types of metal roofing materials include steel, aluminum, and copper. Steel roofing is robust and often treated to resist rust. Aluminum is lightweight and resistant to corrosion, making it ideal for coastal areas. Copper, while expensive, offers a unique aesthetic that many homeowners find appealing.

Each type has its strengths. Steel is strong and affordable, while aluminum is ideal for long-term corrosion resistance in harsh weather. Copper provides a timeless elegance but comes with a steeper price tag. Midwest Construction and Remodeling can help you weigh these options during your roofing consultation.

Advantages and Disadvantages

One significant advantage of metal roofing is its longevity, often lasting 50 years or more with proper maintenance. They are also energy-efficient, reflecting solar heat away from your home. Additionally, many metal roofs come in various styles and colors to match your home’s design.

However, metal roofs also have some downsides. They may be noisier during rain or hail unless proper insulation is applied. They can also be more expensive upfront compared to asphalt shingles. Still, the long-term investment can often be worth it, especially in the variable climate of Plymouth.

Cost and Lifespan

The initial cost of metal roofing ranges widely depending on the material selected. Basic steel panels can be more budget-friendly, while copper roofing tends to be on the higher end. Regardless of initial costs, metal roofs pay off over time with their long lifespan and low maintenance needs.

On average, homeowners can expect metal roofs in Plymouth, MN to last around 40 to 70 years, depending on material choice and maintenance. For those looking to invest in long-term durability, brands recommended by Midwest Construction and Remodeling ensure quality and performance.

Local Suppliers and Installers

Finding quality suppliers and installers is crucial when investing in metal roofing. Most local supply shops in Plymouth stock a variety of options. Midwest Construction and Remodeling partners with trusted suppliers in the area to provide homeowners with the best choices in materials.

The company also works with experienced local installers who know the peculiarities of Plymouth’s weather. They ensure the installation is executed with precision, giving you peace of mind about the loud, chilly Minnesota winters and sweltering summers.

Wood Shakes and Shingles

Differences Between Shakes and Shingles

Wood shakes and shingles are beautiful roofing options that add natural charm to homes. Shakes are hand-split and thicker, providing a rustic feel. Shingles are machine-cut, producing a more uniform and refined look. This distinction affects not only the appearance but also the installation techniques.

Choosing between them depends on the look you desire for your Plymouth home. If you are leaning towards a rustic aesthetic, shakes may be the right choice. However, if you prefer a more polished finish, shingles could be better. Midwest Construction and Remodeling can help you decide based on your preferences and needs.

Durability and Maintenance

When it comes to durability, both shakes and shingles can last for many years, but they do require regular maintenance. Wood roofs must be treated to resist water, insects, and rot. Although they may not hold up as well against severe weather as metal or asphalt, many homeowners appreciate their classic aesthetic.

In Plymouth's variable climate, regular inspections are essential. Midwest Construction and Remodeling offers maintenance services to ensure your wood roof remains in good shape. Regular cleaning and treatments can significantly prolong the life of your wood roofing.

Environmental Impact

One of the significant advantages of wood roofing is its environmental impact. Wood is a renewable resource, making it an eco-friendly option. Sustainable harvesting of timber contributes to less damage to the environment compared to synthetic materials.

However, it’s worth noting that not all wood roofing is created equal. Selecting sustainably sourced woods can make a big difference in its environmental footprint. Midwestern Construction and Remodeling emphasizes responsible sourcing, helping you make a positive choice for the planet.

Preferred Wood Types in Plymouth, MN

Common types of wood used for roofs in Plymouth include cedar and redwood. Cedar is especially favored due to its natural resistance to decay and insects. Redwood also offers similar benefits, along with an attractive appearance that many love.

When choosing wood for roofing, consider climate conditions and your desired aesthetic. Midwest Construction and Remodeling can offer insight into local preferences and help you make a well-informed decision.

Slate Roofing

Natural vs. Synthetic Slate

Slate roofing is an elegant choice many homeowners in Plymouth appreciate. Natural slate is quarried from the Earth, giving each tile a unique coloring and texture. On the other hand, synthetic slate mimics the appearance of natural slate but is made from composite materials.

The choice between natural and synthetic primarily comes down to budget and desired authenticity. Natural slate is more expensive but often regarded as more beautiful. Midwest Construction and Remodeling can provide options suitable for your taste and budget.

Aesthetic Appeal and Customization

When it comes to aesthetics, slate roofing stands out. Its natural beauty and variety of colors add significant character to any home. Luckily, homeowners can choose various colors and thicknesses to match their style.

Whether you’re looking for dark hues or light grays, slate offers versatility to achieve your desired look. Midwest Construction and Remodeling can assist in customizing your slate roofing to enhance your home’s curb appeal further.

Installation Challenges

Installing slate roofing can be a bit tricky due to its weight and fragility. Special care must be taken to ensure the roof structure can support it. Each slate tile must be carefully placed and secured, so proper installation is critical.

With experience in slate installation, Midwest Construction and Remodeling ensures that each project is structured for success. Their professional touch translates into a stunning and durable roof that won’t easily crack or shift.

Longevity and Value

Slate roofs are known for their extraordinary longevity, often surpassing 100 years with proper maintenance. While the initial investment can be high, homeowners can appreciate long-term value. It’s an attractive selling point for potential buyers should you ever decide to move.

This durability makes slate roofing one of the most cost-effective materials over time. Those considering slate roofing should consult with Midwest Construction and Remodeling for realistic expectations and maintenance tips to protect their investment.

Tile Roofing

Clay vs. Concrete Tiles

Tile roofing is well-suited for homes in Plymouth, MN, offering both durability and style. Two common types are clay tiles and concrete tiles. Clay tiles are known for their traditional look and long-lasting performance. They resist moisture and temperatures well, making them perfect for various climates.

Concrete tiles, while heavier, also provide similar durability. They can be molded into different shapes and colors, giving homeowners flexibility in design. Both options are excellent for homeowners who value strength and aesthetics in their roofing choice.

Energy Efficiency

One of the standout features of tile roofing is its energy efficiency. Both clay and concrete tiles reflect UV rays, helping to keep homes cooler in summer months. This can lead to lower energy costs for cooling.

For homeowners in Plymouth, this can translate into significant savings over time. At Midwest Construction and Remodeling, we often highlight the energy-saving potential of tile roofs, helping clients make informed choices for efficiency.

Weight Considerations

Before choosing tile roofing, consider its weight. Both clay and concrete tiles are heavier than other roofing materials. Thus, your roof structure must be evaluated to ensure it can bear the load.

Midwest Construction and Remodeling offers roofing assessments to determine if your home is suitable for tile. They focus on ensuring structural integrity and safety, giving you peace of mind with your installation.

Local Trends and Preferences

Local preferences in Plymouth lean towards tile roofing for many upscale homes. The architectural beauty of tile adds elegance, and the durability fits with Minnesota's changing weather. Homeowners appreciate the long-term investment in resilient roofing materials.

Midwest Construction and Remodeling stays updated on these trends and offers guidance on types and styles, ensuring your roof aligns with contemporary preferences while maintaining its classic appeal.

Rubber Roofing

EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer)

Rubber roofing, particularly EPDM, is a common option for flat roofs in Plymouth, MN. It’s lightweight, durable, and offers excellent water resistance. EPDM roofs can last over 30 years with minimal maintenance.

Many homeowners choose EPDM because of its ease of installation and repair. Furthermore, it can be applied quickly, making it ideal for those who need an immediate solution. Midwest Construction and Remodeling frequently recommends EPDM for flat-roof applications due to its efficiency and longevity.

Thermoplastic Options (TPO, PVC)

Beyond EPDM, there are other rubber options like TPO (Thermoplastic Olefin) and PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) roofs. TPO is known for its reflective properties, making it highly energy-efficient. PVC roofing is durable and resistant to chemicals, making it ideal for commercial applications.

Choosing between these options depends on specific needs, such as energy efficiency and material longevity. Midwest Construction and Remodeling provides guidance on the pros and cons of each material, helping homeowners select the most suitable choice.

Flat Roof Applications

Rubber roofing is a popular choice for flat roofs, given its water resistance and flexibility. It creates a seamless layer, preventing leaks that can come from joints in other materials.

In Plymouth, flat roofs are often less prone to damage if constructed correctly. Midwest Construction and Remodeling specializes in installing flat roofs, ensuring your investment is protected and performs well against the elements.

Quality and Performance in Plymouth Climate

In terms of performance, rubber roofing excels in Plymouth’s diverse climate. It withstands cold winters and hot summers effectively. Furthermore, it requires less maintenance compared to traditional roofing materials, making it a time-saving choice as well.

Midwest Construction and Remodeling is committed to using high-quality rubber roofing materials. Their team ensures installation is done correctly, maximizing the lifespan and performance of your roof. Homeowners can rest easy knowing they have a reliable roofing solution for years to come.
